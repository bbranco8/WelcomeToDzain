1ST SEMESTER

drawing and representation

This subject will alow you to explore the accentuation of graphic expressiveness through different graphic solutions. Furthermore, it will be possible for you to experiment different representation pratics.

The final assessment is based of the conceptual and technical skills acquired and the work carried out by you. Therefore, the final evaluation will take into account Participation - 25% - and Practical Work - 75%. There is no exam but be aware! You must attend at least 75% of both practical and theoretical classes or your assessment will be compromised.

mathematical foundations

The purpose of this subject is to introduce some basic but fundamental mathematical concepts to a scientific approach to design and the connection between digital technology and design. Important: for this subject you should have basic knowledge of mathematics from 9th grade.

There are two possible evaluation methods: a continuous assessment that corresponds to an Assignment (6 points) and an Evaluation Form (14 points), or the Appeal Exam (20 points).

introduction to programing & problem solving

This subject's goal is to allow you to master basic programming concepts, so you will be able to understand and create programs of small/medium complexity. Therefore, it is needed to know the characteristics and syntax of a programming language and to develop problem-solving techniques.

The assessment model of this subject is divided in two components: Continuous Assessment (10 points) and Final Exam with consultation (10 points). The continuous assessment includes a presentation about a Processing project, fortnightly reflections, your performance in classes, a test (2.5 points) and a Project - groups of 2 students with defense (5 points).

typography

The basis of this subject is the understanding of the role of words and text as visual communication instruments. Thus, by learning the fundamental typographic principles, you will be able to develop skills in terms of recognition and selection of different typographic families and different text formats.

The final assessement corresponds to the average of the theoretical assessment - Exam (20 points) - and a continuous practical assessment - exercises with different quotations (20 points) -, each of which cannot be lower than 8 points.
