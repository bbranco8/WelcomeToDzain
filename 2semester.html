2ND SEMESTER

drawing and composition

In this subject will be carry out conceptual, plastic and graphic experimentations. You will acquire visual composition strategies; formal and conceptual analysis skills between drawing in contemporary art and contemporary illustration; and transformation and fusion capabilities between analogue and digital recording.

The final evaluation is divided in two components: Continuous Assessment (25%) - focus on factors inherent to the student's participation - and Pratical Work's Assessment (75%) - focus on factors inherent to the development of 4 exercises.

history of design

The purpose of this subject is to introduce the discipline of graphic design through the study of its history. You should be able to identify major periods in the history of design and the influences that ideas, processes and technologies have on contemporary design.

The assessment consists of a Theoretical Assessment, through a written exam, and an Assessment of Practical Work carried out in groups. Each of the components is worth 50% of the final grade, none of which can be lower than 8 points. Be aware! Attendance at the PLs is mandatory and part of the continuous assessment.

design and communication theory

In this subject is, Design is understood as a discipline that offers communication solutions. By exploring your abilities to think, write, draw, speak and design, you will acquire the tools to respond to audiovisual communication problems.

The theoretical assessment is carried out through a written Exam (20 points). The practical assessment is continuous, based around the execution of three non-repeatable exercises. The exercises have equal quotations that will be added to a component of continuous assessment - attendance, achievement and participation in classes - totaling 20 points. The final grade is the average of the two assessments - theoretical + practical -, each of which may not be less than 8 points.

multimedia programming

This subject's goal is to develop programming skills involving multimedia elements, as well as modeling, simulation and animation of graphical representations, and the dynamic data structures necessary for this. You will be able to develop medium-sized multimedia projects.

The assessment model of this subject is divided in three components: Exam with consultation - non-digital documentation only - (8 points), Continuous Assessment (4 points), and Mini-project - groups of two - (8 points). Approval conditions: minimum of 35% in continuous assessment + mini-project, minimum 35% in the exam and 9.5 values in the sum of the two components.
